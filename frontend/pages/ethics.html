AI Ethics & Legal Framework
NyayaAI is built on a foundation of responsible AI principles, strict legal accuracy, and transparency about the limitations of AI-assisted legal education.
Core AI Ethics Principles
⚖
Legal Accuracy First
All AI responses are grounded in BNS/BNSS/BSA bare act text via RAG retrieval. No hallucinated citations.
🚫
Bias Prevention
The AI judge applies consistent standards regardless of the student's role, college, or argument style.
🔍
Transparency
Every evaluation point links to the exact legal provision it references. No opaque scoring.

BNS / BNSS / BSA — Operative Legal Framework

NyayaAI exclusively uses India's new criminal law framework enacted in 2023–2024. References to the old IPC, CrPC, or IEA are never used.

AI Model Disclosure

NyayaAI uses Anthropic Claude Sonnet as its underlying language model for generating judge responses, opposing counsel arguments, witness testimony, and performance evaluations.

Legal citations are retrieved via ChromaDB RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) from indexed BNS/BNSS/BSA bare act text. This significantly reduces hallucination risk for legal citations.

The AI judge operates with an ethics constraint that prevents it from generating content that glorifies crime, provides real legal advice, or references superseded laws.

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, NyayaAI has the following known limitations:

  • AI-generated legal citations may occasionally be inaccurate. Always verify against the official bare act.
  • The AI judge's rulings are simulated and do not reflect actual judicial reasoning.
  • Complex procedural nuances of Indian criminal law may not be fully captured.
  • The system does not account for state-specific amendments or local court practices.
  • Performance scores are heuristic-based and should not be used for formal assessment.
